Where We Started: Facing the Carbon Reality

In 2021, Giant Storage commissioned an independent carbon audit of our entire operation — from raw material sourcing through to end-of-life product disposal across our customer supply chains. The results were sobering but instructive: our Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions totalled 42,800 tonnes of CO₂-equivalent annually, while our Scope 3 value chain emissions were estimated at four times that figure.

That audit became the foundation of our Net Zero 2030 programme — not a marketing exercise, but a systematic, engineering-driven decarbonization roadmap with independently verified milestones and public accountability. This article describes where we are in that journey and what our customers can expect from us through to 2030.

Solar Integration: Powering Manufacturing with Renewables

The single largest contributor to our direct emissions was our manufacturing energy consumption. Our flagship Cairo facility operates 24 injection moulding presses, air compression systems, and climate-controlled production bays — an energy-intensive combination that historically relied entirely on grid power with Egypt's fossil-fuel-weighted generation mix.

In 2023, we completed Phase 1 of our rooftop solar installation: 4,800 photovoltaic panels covering 14,000m² of production hall roofing. The system produces 3.2 MW of peak capacity, meeting 35% of our total manufacturing energy needs on optimal days and averaging 28% across the full year. Phase 2, currently in procurement, will extend coverage to our logistics and warehousing areas, targeting 55% solar self-sufficiency by end-2025.

"Sustainability and competitiveness are not in tension — they are increasingly the same thing. Our solar investment has delivered an 18% reduction in energy cost per unit while cutting our manufacturing carbon intensity by 22%. The business case wrote itself." — Sarah Al-Mansouri, Head of Sustainability

Electric Fleet Transition

Our logistics operations — including factory-floor material handling, finished goods marshalling, and local distribution — represent our second-largest emission source. In 2022, our vehicle fleet consisted of 34 internal combustion forklifts and 8 diesel trucks for short-haul distribution.

By end-2024, we will have completed the transition of all factory-floor material handling to electric battery-powered forklifts, charged overnight from our solar capacity. This alone eliminates 1,850 tonnes of CO₂ per year from our direct emission inventory. The local distribution fleet transition to electric vehicles is scheduled for 2025–2026, pending the maturation of EV truck payload capacity to match our operational requirements.

Circular Material Economy: Recycled Content at Scale

Our products — pallets, crates, and storage bins — are manufactured from polyolefin polymers that can be recycled without quality degradation through multiple reprocessing cycles. This creates the foundation for a true circular economy model, where end-of-life Giant Storage products become the raw material for new ones.

In 2023, we launched our Take-Back Programme, offering customers a logistics-simple product return mechanism at the point of repurchase. Returned products are granulated at our facility and blended back into production at 20–40% loading, depending on product specification. Our Life Cycle Assessment confirms a 38% reduction in cradle-to-gate carbon per unit for our recycled-content product range, with zero compromise on structural performance.

  • Over 850 tonnes of post-use plastic recovered and reprocessed in 2023
  • Recycled-content products now represent 31% of our standard product range
  • Target: 60% of standard range to incorporate recycled content by 2026
  • Zero landfill from production waste since Q3 2023

Supplier Engagement: Decarbonizing the Value Chain

Our Scope 3 emissions — those generated by our suppliers and customers — represent 80% of our total carbon footprint. Addressing them requires partnerships, not just policies. In 2024, we launched the Giant Storage Supplier Sustainability Charter, requiring our top-50 suppliers by spend to submit verified carbon reduction targets by January 2025 and to report quarterly progress.

Suppliers meeting Bronze, Silver, or Gold charter standards receive preferential payment terms, priority in our preferred supplier programme, and co-marketing support. Three suppliers who achieved Gold standard in our pilot cohort reported an average of 19% reduction in their own manufacturing emissions — a benefit that extends well beyond their relationship with us.

Our 2030 Targets: Measurable, Verified, Public

Our Net Zero 2030 commitment is structured around five independently verified milestones. We publish progress reports quarterly through our sustainability microsite and submit to third-party verification by Bureau Veritas annually.

  • 2024: 35% renewable energy in manufacturing; all factory forklifts electric
  • 2025: 55% renewable energy; 40% recycled content in standard range
  • 2026: Local distribution fleet fully electric; Scope 1+2 emissions cut by 55%
  • 2028: Scope 1+2 net zero through remaining renewable offset; Scope 3 reduction programme at 40%
  • 2030: Full Net Zero across verified Scope 1, 2, and key Scope 3 categories

What This Means for Our Customers

Increasingly, our customers' own sustainability commitments — driven by regulation, investor pressure, and consumer expectations — require them to account for the embedded carbon in their supply chain inputs. Giant Storage's sustainability programme directly reduces that embedded carbon, providing customers with verified Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs) for our key product lines.

We are proud that our sustainability journey benefits not just our own operations, but contributes meaningfully to the decarbonization of the global supply chains we serve. We invite our customers and partners to join us in this commitment, and our sustainability team is always available to discuss collaborative carbon reduction initiatives.
